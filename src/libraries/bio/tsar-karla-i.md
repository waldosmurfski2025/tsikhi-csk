**Tsar Karla I** (Cossackian: Карла Эдуардович Соёков; better known as **Karla the Great**; 9 November 1696 – 19 December 1764) was the fifth Tsar of Cossackia. He led the social reforms that established the social welfare, political maneuvering, and the medical advancements of the country.

## The Poltare War

Cossackia's frontier near Wallachia were being raided by remnant Pagan knights, named the Poltares. They used to be a Christian group, but were excommunicated for their beliefs on the Holy Trinity. They also tried to become an Orthodox sect, but were also condemned as heretical. Rejected by their Christian faith, they turned back to Pagan religion, and swore to wreak destruction on all Christian civilization. The Papal State at the time had already posted a Papal Edict calling to eradicate the Poltares. Cossackia, at that time, was in a perfect position to fight the Poltares. Cossackia is a Roman Catholic country (with major Eastern Orthodox support), with a military modern in its time thanks to the reforms by the Eduardian Tsars.
